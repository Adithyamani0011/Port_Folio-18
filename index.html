Bill Gates

Bill Gates (born October 28, 1955, Seattle, Washington, U.S.) American computer programmer and entrepreneur who cofounded Microsoft Corporation, the world’s largest personal-computer software company.

Microsoft and the PC Revolution: In 1975, Gates left Harvard during his junior year and, along with Allen, founded Microsoft.1975– When Bill started Microsoft, their vision of “a computer on every desktop and in every home” seemed farfetched to most people. Today, thanks to Microsoft and many other companies, that vision is a reality in many parts of the world, and personal technology is an integral part of society.

By the 1990s, Gates had become the PC industry’s ultimate kingmaker. Bill Gates cofounded Microsoft Corporation, the world’s largest personal-computer software company.

Awards

Presidential Medal of Freedom: In 2016, President Barack Obama awarded Bill Gates the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

Saturn Award: Gates received a special award at the 1995 Saturn Awards for his contributions to science fiction films, particularly for his work with computer-generated imagery (CGI) technology through Microsoft.
